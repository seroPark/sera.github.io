MindShare

MindShare is a mobile application that empowers individuals to comfortably voice their emotional and mental wellbeing. By addressing the stigma surrounding mental health, the app offers reliable support and resources, alongside credible information and education, to foster a more informed and compassionate society.

Problem Statement

Societal, social and cultural stigmas surrounding mental health makes it difficult for individuals to communicate their struggles with emotional and mental well-being. Resulting barriers to communication limit onesâ€™ access to mental health resources, further exacerbating their mental-health situation.

Questions

  • How can we enable individual comfortability when it comes to voicing out about emotional and mental wellbeing?
  • How do we enable improved accessibility to proper mental health resources for those in need?
  • How do we tear down societal, social and cultural stigmatisation of mental health issues?

Methods

To gain a deeper understanding of the problem space and inform our design process, we employed three research methods: primary research, literature review, and case studies. Each method was carefully chosen to explore different facets of the research question and ensure a well-rounded approach to data collection.

Primary Research

Each team member conducted individual interviews on the topic, gathering qualitative insights into various experiences and perspectives . These findings were synthesised to refine our group's problem statement and align our objectives.

Literature Review (Secondary Research)

A literature review was conducted to gather insights from published sources on social stigma, mental health, community cohesion, and mental health education. By analysing and synthesising this data, we developed a theoretical foundation to guide our design decisions and identify key challenges within the space.

Case Studies

Case studies of existing platforms and solutions were performed to evaluate their strengths and weaknesses. This analysis helped us identify gaps and opportunities in the current landscape, enabling us to focus on areas that are often overlooked.

Key Findings

Primary Research

Quantitative

  • 72.1% of the respondents rated the mental health literacy of the general public a 3 and below on a scale of 1 to 5. (1 being the least literate)
  • 62.8% of respondents stated that they were not comfortable with sharing the state of their mental wellbeing with their family.
  • 69.7% of respondents stated that they were never taught the importance of mental wellbeing growing up.

Qualitative

As mentioned in the narrowing of our problem scenario, our research showed a sizeable amount of individuals faced issues when it came to comfortability of sharing about their mental and emotional issues.

Literature Review

Key Benefits

  • Many users avoid seeking offline help due to stigma but find comfort in the relatable and non-judgemental nature of online mental health forums (Smith-Merry et al., 2019).
  • Online communities promote positive cognitive changes in individuals with mental illness (De Choudhury & Kiciman, 2017).
  • Participation in these forums has been shown to reduce suicidal thoughts (Pruksachatkun et al., 2019).

Challenges

  • Mental health forums can expose users to triggering content, posing potential risks (Smith-Merry et al., 2019).
  • Users may struggle to maintain personal boundaries, often hesitating to post out of concern for negatively affecting others or being impacted themselves (Smith-Merry et al., 2019).

Usability Test

Once we had our initial mock up, feedback was gathered through user-based testing with parents and children and expert-based testing involving fellow students, tutors, and lecturers. This rigorous process enabled us to identify pain points, refine interactions, and improve the overall user experience.

Key Usability Findings

Interactive Storybook

Strengths

  • Clarity & Accessibility: Clear, well-segmented content with simple language for younger audiences.
  • Interactivity: Flashing icons and transitions fostered curiosity and excitement.
  • Educational Value: Effectively addressed gaps in traditional sex education with foundational knowledge.

Areas for Improvements

  • Interactive Consistency: Similar visual elements with varying interactivity caused confusion.
  • Feedback Mechanisms: Lack of feedback for incorrect answers reduced learning impact.
  • Content Depth: Some sections were text-heavy or overly complex, overwhelming younger users.

Mobile Application

Strengths

  • User Interface Design: Clean and user-friendly layout with appealing visuals.
  • Content Presentation: Bite-sized summaries effectively delivered key topics.
  • Parental Motivation: Highlighted gaps in users' own sex education and inspired proactive learning.

Areas for Improvements

  • Content Appropriateness: Certain topics were too explicit or shallow for younger audiences.
  • Confusing Interactivity: Icons and navigation lacked clarity, particularly for call-to-action elements.
  • Instruction & Feedback: Users wanted clearer guidance on integrating the app with the storybook and addressing sensitive topics.
  • Customization Features: Poor understanding of personalisation options led to confusion about their necessity.

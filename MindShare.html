MindShare

MindShare is a mobile application that empowers individuals to comfortably voice their emotional and mental wellbeing. By addressing the stigma surrounding mental health, the app offers reliable support and resources, alongside credible information and education, to foster a more informed and compassionate society.

Problem Statement

Societal, social and cultural stigmas surrounding mental health makes it difficult for individuals to communicate their struggles with emotional and mental well-being. Resulting barriers to communication limit ones’ access to mental health resources, further exacerbating their mental-health situation.

Questions

  • How can we enable individual comfortability when it comes to voicing out about emotional and mental wellbeing?
  • How do we enable improved accessibility to proper mental health resources for those in need?
  • How do we tear down societal, social and cultural stigmatisation of mental health issues?

Methods

To gain a deeper understanding of the problem space and inform our design process, we employed three research methods: primary research, literature review, and case studies. Each method was carefully chosen to explore different facets of the research question and ensure a well-rounded approach to data collection.

Primary Research

Each team member conducted individual interviews on the topic, gathering qualitative insights into various experiences and perspectives . These findings were synthesised to refine our group's problem statement and align our objectives.

Literature Review (Secondary Research)

A literature review was conducted to gather insights from published sources on social stigma, mental health, community cohesion, and mental health education. By analysing and synthesising this data, we developed a theoretical foundation to guide our design decisions and identify key challenges within the space.

Case Studies

Case studies of existing platforms and solutions were performed to evaluate their strengths and weaknesses. This analysis helped us identify gaps and opportunities in the current landscape, enabling us to focus on areas that are often overlooked.

Key Findings

Primary Research

Quantitative

  • 72.1% rated public mental health literacy low (3 or below out of 5)
  • 62.8% felt uncomfortable sharing mental health concerns with family.
  • 69.7% were never taught the importance of mental wellbeing.

Qualitative

Our research showed a sizeable amount of individuals faced issues when it came to comfortability of sharing about their mental and emotional issues.

Literature Review

Key Benefits

  • Online mental health forums offer comfort through relatable, non-judgemental spaces.
  • Online communities promote positive cognitive changes in individuals with mental illness
  • Participation in forums has been shown to reduce suicidal thoughts and behaviours.

Challenges

  • Mental health forums can expose users to triggering content, posing potential risks.
  • Users may struggle to maintain personal boundaries, often hesitating to post out of concern for negatively affecting others or being impacted themselves.

Proposed Concept

Our key findings paved the way to identifying our key user needs, we ideated and settled on three crucial features which our platform will be built upon, with the intent of addressing said user needs.

User Needs

Comfortability was a reoccurring theme throughout our research process, with prevalence in both our primary and secondary data sources showed. This is the primary user need our solution aims to target.

Lack of reliable mental health support was another common issue identified, with our primary & secondary data sources showing that even seeking professional mental health services in person can have adverse effects.

Low mental health literacy and a prevalence of societal and culture induced stigmas surrounding mental health were identified as root causes of barriers to proper mental health support. Credible information and education would stand to disperse stigma and misconceptions in the space, and enable improved mental health literacy.

Proposed Features

The versatile nature of a community based platform makes this our primary feature. With the feature being build around user to user interactions, we thought that with proper moderation and facilitation of the space, it has the ability to address all three user needs with proper execution. Extensive literature review and case studies has further validated this claim as well.

By having industry certified professionals providing personalised support to our users, this addresses the need of providing reliable mental health support to the user. The professionals are also in charge of moderating the community platforms, enabling safe, reliable, trigger free content on our platform.

Our educational hub will feature peer reviewed podcasts, articles, research papers and videos from trusted sources. This feature will enhance mental health literacy, fulfilling the need for credible information and education.

Prototype - Iteration 1

Our initial prototype focused on testing the platform's core features, particularly the community and user-to-user interactions. Expert-based evaluation was conducted first to address minor usability issues, ensuring a smoother experience before testing with target users. This iteration provided valuable insights to refine the design and better align with user needs.

Expert-Based: Heuristics Evaluation

We conducted heuristic evaluations to identify key usability issues and synthesised findings into actionable items. Required documents—Consent Form, Heuristic Template, Tasks, and Prototype link—were organised in Figma and duplicated for each evaluator to avoid influence from others' feedback.

  • Step.1 Each evaluator received their unique Figma file at the start of the session.
  • Step.2 Evaluators who consented to recording signed the consent form, and the session was recorded for reference.
  • Step.3 In cases of time constraints (30-minute sessions), missing details were verbally discussed and later filled in by the facilitator using the recording.
  • Step.4 Evaluators completed the heuristic template, rating each violation from 0–4 severity (see Appendix A-2, A-4).
  • Step.5 Evaluators explored the prototype using 6 structured tasks, e.g., "Task 6: Create a new forum post."

Key Findings

  • Issue #1 No sources to help users to understand the app’s features in a sensitive mental health community platform.
  • Issue #2: Not enough options presented for users throughout the app (including media files, dropdown & filtering).
  • Issue #3: Users are overwhelmed at the clustered look of the page from visual elements to small texts.
  • Issue #4: Users are unsure about whether their posting has been successful, where their post will go & who can see their post.
  • Issue #5: Many features/elements are repetitive and confuses users.

Prototype - Iteration 2

User-Based Testing

To gain deeper insights into how users interact with the refined prototype, we used a triangulation approach that combined observation, interviews, and surveys. This method revealed both usability issues and opportunities for improvement by capturing user behaviour, thoughts, and feedback.

Step.1 : Session Preparation

  • Conducted in a controlled environment (in-person or online).
  • Setup included a facilitator, an observer, and one user per session.
  • Recorded sessions if time constraints arose for later analysis.

Step.2 : Think Aloud Protocol

  • Users verbalised their thoughts while completing tasks, highlighting micro-struggles and usability issues (Nielsen, 2021).

Step.3 : Task Scenarios

  • Users completed 8 scenarios, such as creating posts, finding resources, or booking sessions, to simulate real-world app usage.

Step.4 : SUS Survey

  • Post-session, users completed a 10-question System Usability Scale (SUS) survey (Likert scale) to rate usability.

Step.5 : Interviews

  • Pre-session warm-ups eased participants into the mental health topic.
  • Post-session interviews probed deeper into SUS ratings and user experiences.

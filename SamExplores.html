Sam Explores

Sam Explores was conceptualised to tackle the global challenge of delivering comprehensive sexual education. Guided by the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goal 4 (Quality Education), our team developed an accessible and engaging tool to equip children and their parents with the knowledge and skills essential for healthy development.

Research Objective

To explore the dynamics of parent-child conversations about sex-related topics by investigating the diverse needs, desires and challenges experienced by parents and children. The study also aims to discover insights that can contribute to the development of effective strategies and resources for promoting open and informative discussions about sex-related subjects.

Questions

  • How might early sexuality education equip children with practical knowledge for navigating real-life situations they may be confronted with?
  • What are the primary barriers preventing parents from engaging in open discussions about holistic sexual education with their children, and what effective strategies exist for overcoming these obstacles?
  • How can we enhance parental knowledge and understanding of the value of comprehensive sexuality education in early childhood, particularly in the context of cultural and social barriers?

Methods

To gain a deep understanding of the problem space and user needs, we conducted comprehensive research through three distinct methods: questionnaires, online ethnography, and interviews. Each method was chosen to address specific aspects of the research question while capturing diverse perspectives.

Questionnaire

An online survey was designed to gather a broad, quantitative overview of attitudes towards sexual education. This included exploring topics such as access to resources, comfort in discussing sensitive topics, and existing knowledge gaps.

Online Ethnography

By observing discussions on platforms such as Reddit, Twitter, and ReachOut, we explored unfiltered conversations around sexual education. This method allowed us to identify recurring themes, cultural attitudes, and the influence of stigma or shame in online communities.

Interviews

We conducted in-depth interviews with parents and adolescents to explore personal experiences, challenges, and desires for sexual education. These conversations provided nuanced, qualitative insights into user behaviours and unmet needs.

Analysis

To analyse research findings into actionable insights, we utilised an affinity diagram to identify patterns and prioritise key themes. This process helped us transform raw data into a structured understanding of user needs and challenges.

Key Findings

  • Cultural and religious conservatism fosters discomfort and stigma.
  • Parents and adolescents struggle to initiate discussions due to embarrassment or uncertainty.
  • Taboo topics, such as LGBTQ+ inclusion, are rarely addressed in conversations or educational materials.
  • Adolescents primarily rely on schools, peers, and online platforms for sexual education.
  • Parents are often excluded from this process or see it as the school’s responsibility.
  • Misinformation from unverified sources perpetuates myths and misunderstandings.
  • Many parents lack the confidence or knowledge to discuss sexual education effectively.
  • They express a need for accessible, reliable resources to guide conversations.
  • Concerns about the “right timing” and approach create hesitation to engage in discussions.
  • Current educational materials fail to adequately address diverse experiences, including those of LGBTQ+ individuals.
  • Adolescents desire inclusive, judgment-free environments to discuss sensitive topics.
  • Lack of representation perpetuates feelings of exclusion for marginalised groups.
  • Providing parents with tools and resources can empower them to have meaningful discussions with their children.
  • Schools can play a larger role by fostering open, inclusive dialogues and involving parents.
  • Normalising ongoing, age-appropriate conversations about sexual health is crucial for long-term impact.

Synthesis

Building on the insights gathered, we synthesised the data through user personas and journey maps. These enabled us to dive deeper into user experiences, highlighting pain points and identifying opportunities for improvement.

Ideation

Collaborative Storyboarding & Decision Matrix

We utilised storyboarding to present and evaluate diverse concepts, collaboratively analysing their feasibility, strengths, and areas for improvement. To identify the most promising concept, we utilised a decision matrix with six key criteria.

  1. Supports Continuous Learning: Encourages ongoing Comprehensive Sexuality Education for parents and adolescents.
  2. Facilitates Open Dialogue: Promotes meaningful conversations about sex-related topics between parents and children.
  3. Accessibility and Inclusivity: Accommodates diverse social, cultural, and economic backgrounds.
  4. Age Appropriateness: Tailored to the developmental stages of children.
  5. Evidence-Based Content: Offers reliable tools and resources on sexual education.
  6. Privacy and Data Safety: Protects user data and ensures confidentiality.

Concept: Sam Explores

We combined the best elements of the top concepts to create the final design: Sam Explores. This solution integrates interactive reading features with tools that foster open parent-child conversations. The result is an inclusive, engaging, and educational tool designed to meet the diverse needs of users.

Usability Test

Once we had our initial mock up, feedback was gathered through user-based testing with parents and children and expert-based testing involving fellow students, tutors, and lecturers. This rigorous process enabled us to identify pain points, refine interactions, and improve the overall user experience.

Key Usability Findings

Interactive Storybook

Strengths

  • Clarity & Accessibility: Clear, well-segmented content with simple language for younger audiences.
  • Interactivity: Flashing icons and transitions fostered curiosity and excitement.
  • Educational Value: Effectively addressed gaps in traditional sex education with foundational knowledge.

Areas for Improvements

  • Interactive Consistency: Similar visual elements with varying interactivity caused confusion.
  • Feedback Mechanisms: Lack of feedback for incorrect answers reduced learning impact.
  • Content Depth: Some sections were text-heavy or overly complex, overwhelming younger users.

Mobile Application

Strengths

  • User Interface Design: Clean and user-friendly layout with appealing visuals.
  • Content Presentation: Bite-sized summaries effectively delivered key topics.
  • Parental Motivation: Highlighted gaps in users' own sex education and inspired proactive learning.

Areas for Improvements

  • Content Appropriateness: Certain topics were too explicit or shallow for younger audiences.
  • Confusing Interactivity: Icons and navigation lacked clarity, particularly for call-to-action elements.
  • Instruction & Feedback: Users wanted clearer guidance on integrating the app with the storybook and addressing sensitive topics.
  • Customization Features: Poor understanding of personalisation options led to confusion about their necessity.
